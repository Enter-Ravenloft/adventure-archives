The Narrator [3PuttMutt] BOT 29-12-2025 18:10
The common room at the docks of Lake Zarovich is little more than a repurposed storehouse, its wide doors left open to the water to keep smoke from lingering beneath the low rafters. A hearth burns against the far wall, doing more for the habit of it than the warmth it provides. Behind a rough plank bar stands a middle-aged woman with broad shoulders and tired eyes. She moves about by rote memorization - someone who has been doing this alone for a long time. She doesn't look up as you enter, but the gathered patrons do. Adventurers are rare here, and even more rarely welcome. The looks that come from the men and women scattered amongst the tables are equal parts wonder and disdain - a common treatment from Barovian locals. Adventurers mean trouble, and most Barovians spend their time limiting their exposure to it. As such, the mood here is pensive. Evening sails on the lake are uncommon, given the dangers that lurk in the mists and its depths. The gathered crowd is not your workaday sailor - the usuals like Fisherman Kai have long gone home and locked their doors for the night. This is the second shift - men and women with something to prove... or nothing to lose. At the table closest to the hearth, the man at the center is unmistakable even without introduction. Captain Ilya Markovic looks exactly like you'd expect a lake captain to look: weathered face, close-cropped beard with gray specks, heavy coat wrapped around him like permanent attire. Four others sit with him, clearly sailors by their clothes and demeanor. They are engaged in a conversation that seems tactical. A second table nearby holds another group of humans finishing their ales. They are a little more rough-looking and jovial. Chatter and laughter rise above a dice game that has begun, with piles of coin in denominations that most adventurers would spend without thought. One of them glances toward the windows often, as if he expected to see something through the mist. (edited)
