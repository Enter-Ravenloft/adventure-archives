# Chasing the Light 4 Word had spread. Sunlight! Sunlight in Barovia! Certainly there had been the rare occasion when a priest of Lanthander had channeled all of their divinity for a few scant moments of radiant dawn, or a mage who had burnt years of magical reserves to produce a concentrated ray of sunlight. And there were tales that some in this land could show the past, a past in which the cloud had not yet blotted out the sun. But here was an island, in the far distance on Lake Zarovich, barely visible from the ahore, that had seen sunlight for minutes at a time, multiple days in a row. In a land of dark shadows, populated and ruled by darker creatures, sunlight was as precious a commodity as silver, and rarer. And of course the local authorities would get involved, eapecially after the first few expeditions had proved treacherous. Adventurers had gone on their own and had met with varied levels of misfortune, but none as yet had learned--or at least announced that they had learned--of the source of the sunlight. And there lay opportunity. Between Ismark, burgomaster of Barovia village, and Lady Wachter, burgomaster of Vallaki, whoever was first to discover the nature of the sunlight on the craggy island, they would sure be able to use it, one way or another, to grow their own power. "Coin. Good coin, should you deliver me actionable information," Ismark had told several adventurers before sending them on their way. It was like feeding two crows with one crumb: he might learn something of use from the excursion, while getting several troublemaking adventurers out of his village. -# . (edited)
