Heavy, steady rain obscured the horizon, and the sky roiled with dark clouds. Your destination was a mere two hours away on a good day, but today it took three. The homestead's proximity to the village proper likely played a role in the urgency of the job, and in the substantial reward that success promised. Even when made a sodden mess by the weather, the well-traveled dirt path was not difficult to follow. Mercifully, the rain was already clearing on the approach to the farm. Downpour faded to a drizzle. By the time you arrived, the precipitation had given way to a wispy mist that haunted distant treelines. Leaves glistened with dew. Up ahead, a side road branched off the dirt path, winding between recently-tilled fields before ending in a modest collection of buildings. From where you stood, you spied the broad silhouette of a barn, the circular roof of a small well, and the two stories of a thatched house. But perhaps what was most striking was what wasn't there. The constant white noise of rain had faded away, leaving behind a dead silence. No sound, no bird, no animal, no human voice, could be heard on approach to the farm. The only visible motion was that of slowly rolling mist, the subtle sway of trees, and the four recently-arrived sellswords. Were it not for those, the world would have seemed perfectly still. Regardless, you had a job to do.
